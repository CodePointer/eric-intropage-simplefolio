[Research] Depth reconstruction with neural signed distance fields in structured light systems

You may have heard of Kinect or Realsense, which are depth cameras that can capture images with 3D information. That's what I did in my PhD research. More specifically, I worked on the fundamental techniques for those sensors: structured light systems.

The structured light system is a classical technique for depth sensing, which has been widely used in various applications, such as 3D scanning, robotics, and intelligent manufacturing. In my research, I proposed a quite novel approach to consider this topic from a new perspective: I used neural signed distance fields (NSDF) to represent the 3D shape of the object, and trained a neural work to reconstruct the 3D shape from the captured images. The result is quite promising, since we was the first one who apply this framework to structured light systems.

I won't go much into the details here, but you can find the paper in the link below. (Published in 3DV 2024)

[Research] Online adaptive disparity estimation for dynamic scenes in structured light systems

In this research, I proposed a novel approach for depth estimation in structured light systems targeting dynamic scenes. Different from many deep-learning-based methods, our approach can adaptively adjust the model parameters online, which is crucial for dynamic scenes.

The key idea is to fully utilize the temporal information. The motion can both be challenge and opportunity for depth estimation, and we grab it with a novel flow-based estimation method. The method is able to estimate some sparse flow trajectories, and then use them to adaptively adjust the model parameters. The result is quite promising, since we can achieve real-time performance with high accuracy. (Published in IROS 2023 and RA-Letter 2022)
